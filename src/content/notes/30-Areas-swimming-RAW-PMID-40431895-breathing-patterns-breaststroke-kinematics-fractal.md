# PMID-40431895 — Analyzing Breathing Patterns in the Breaststroke Technique Through Dual-Media Kinematics and Fractal Dimension

**Source:** J Biomech 2024 (same research group as 39486378 — likely related study)

## Abstract

This study aimed to characterize and compare kinematics between two breaststroke breathing patterns — breathing every cycle and breathing every two cycles — while also analyzing intra-cyclic velocity variation (dv) and fractal dimension. Six-week familiarization period was included.

Key findings:
- Minimal differences between three cycle types (breathing, non-breathing, breathing-after-non-breathing)
- **Non-breathing cycle**: had the shallowest and deepest head positions (wider range of motion)
- Results suggest breathing pattern has limited impact on overall kinematics when familiarized
- Fractal dimension analysis shows breathing cycles are more regular/rhythmic than non-breathing cycles

**Interpretation:**
- Head position range is wider in non-breathing cycles (more flexibility)
- Breathing pattern effect on dv is small after familiarization
- **教練意涵**：蛙泳每划一呼吸 vs 每两划一呼吸的選擇應個人化，關鍵是訓練熟悉度而非某種模式本身
