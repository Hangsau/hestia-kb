## Abstract (RAW — English only)

Load-velocity (L-V) profiling via semi-tethered swimming tests (4 sprints: 25m, 25m, 20m, 15m at 0.1, 2.0, 4.0, 6.0kg). Estimated maximal velocity (V0) and body-mass-normalized load (rL0). Race variables: clean swimming speed (V), stroke rate (SR), distance per cycle (SL), stroke index (SI). Results: L-V variables more related to 100m than 200m. Maximal relative load (rL0) strongly correlated with 100m performance (r=0.63); trivial for 200m (r=0.12). SR strongly associated with rL0 (r=0.83). V0 associated with SR in 200m (r=0.68). Conclusions: L-V profile relationships to race variables depend on event distance; more predictive for 100m than 200m.

---

## Key Findings
1. **L-V profiling more relevant to 100m than 200m** — distance matters for interpretation
2. **rL0 (maximal relative load)**: strong correlation with 100m performance (r=0.63), trivial for 200m (r=0.12)
3. **SR ↔ rL0**: very strong (r=0.83) — load-velocity profile predicts stroke rate
4. **V0 ↔ SR in 200m**: strong (r=0.68) — maximum velocity predicts stroke rate in longer event
5. **100m**: proporative/sprint profile drives performance; **200m**: aerobic/v0 profile more relevant
