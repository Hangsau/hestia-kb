## Abstract (RAW — English only)

Aim: To clarify changes in muscle coordination during butterfly swimming at different swimming speeds. Eight female swimmers performed 25m butterfly at 60%, 80%, and 100% effort. Two-dimensional motion analysis + surface EMG from 12 muscles. Muscle synergies identified via non-negative matrix factorisation. Key finding: catch phase of arm stroke shortened with increasing speeds. Four muscle synergies extracted across speeds. Spatial components of 4 synergies (pull, push, recovery, downward kick) were similar across speeds. **Drive timing of the three synergies changed earlier at highest effort level**. Earlier timing in Synergy 1 between 60% and 100% effort was positively correlated with earlier start of pull phase (r = 0.72, p < 0.05). Conclusion: speed-related kinematic changes are linked mainly to shifts in synergy timing, not changes in individual muscle activity.

---

## Key Findings
1. **4 synergies** (pull, push, recovery, downward kick) — spatial pattern stable across all speeds
2. **Spatial components of synergies are speed-invariant** — the same muscle groups activate at all speeds
3. **Timing shifts with speed** — earlier drive timing at higher effort (not different muscle recruitment)
4. **Synergy 1 timing → pull phase start** (r=0.72): the more you advance synergy 1 timing, the earlier your pull begins
5. **Speed ↑ → catch phase shortens** — higher speeds require faster catch execution
6. **Training implication**: training should focus on shifting synergy timing earlier, not recruiting new muscles
