# PMID-39486378 — Exploring the impact of breathing patterns on active drag in breaststroke swimming

**Alves M, Barbosa TM, Paulo Vilas-Boas J** — J Biomech 2024 Dec;177:112390

## Abstract

This study aimed to compare active drag (Da), coefficient of hydrodynamic force (CDa) and total external mechanical power (Ptot) between two breathing patterns in breaststroke: breathing every stroke versus every two strokes. A 6-week intervention program was conducted. Fifteen swimmers carried out two all-out bouts of 25m using the velocity perturbation method in each breathing pattern. The study analyzed swimmers' velocity (swimming freely and with the towed body), Da, CDa and Ptot.

**Key quantified findings:**
- Individual data analysis: 40% of sample experienced a **decrease in Da when breathing every two strokes**, with some achieving higher velocities
- Nine swimmers exhibited **lower Da values while breathing every stroke**, with six of them also showing higher velocity
- **Pooled sample**: no differences in Da, CDa, Ptot between the two breathing patterns (paired t-tests)
- ANCOVA: neither sex nor swimming velocity influenced the variables studied

**Interpretation:**
- Individual response to breathing pattern is highly variable — no universal "optimal" pattern
- The "most hydrodynamic position" is with head submerged (支持減少呼吸頻率)
- Some swimmers perform better with more frequent breathing (individual physiology > general principle)
- 40% benefit from every-two-strokes pattern; others need every-stroke
