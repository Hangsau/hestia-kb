## Abstract (RAW — English only)

### OBJECTIVE
This study investigated the relationship between hydrodynamic characteristics and the selection of backstroke turns among adolescent swimmers.

### METHODS
Ninety swimmers performed turns with three techniques: somersault, open, and bucket. Drag, lift, and propulsive forces were measured.

### RESULTS
The crossover turn produced the highest wall push-off velocity (2.17 ± 0.05 m/s) compared to open and somersault turns. No significant differences were observed between techniques for drag, lift, or propulsive force coefficients.

### CONCLUSIONS
The higher wall push-off velocity in crossover turns may be attributed to more effective force application timing rather than superior hydrodynamic positioning. Training should focus on the execution timing of the wall push-off phase.

---

## Key Findings
1. **Crossover turn**: highest wall push-off velocity (2.17 ± 0.05 m/s)
2. **No hydrodynamic advantage**: drag, lift, propulsive force coefficients were NOT different between techniques
3. **Timing over positioning**: higher push-off velocity attributed to better force application timing, NOT better hydrodynamics
4. **Training implication**: focus on wall push-off execution timing, not just body position
