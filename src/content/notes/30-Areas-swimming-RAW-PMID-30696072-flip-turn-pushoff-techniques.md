# PMID-30696072 — Kinetic Analysis of Swimming Flip-Turn Push-Off Techniques

**Weimar W, Sumner A, Romer B, Fox J, Rehm J, Decoux B, Patel J** — Sports (Basel) 2019 Jan 28;7(2):32

## Abstract

The aims of the present study were to examine the influences of different push-off techniques on kinetic and kinematic parameters both in and out of the water. Two techniques compared:
1. **Rapid extension technique** (no countermovement): rapid extension of knees and hips towards the wall prior to contact
2. **Countermovement technique**: swimmer glides into the wall, letting the wall flex the knees (eccentric phase)

Twenty swimmers participated.

**Key findings:**
- [Full abstract truncated in PubMed; key implications from title and context]
- Two distinct kinetic strategies for flip-turn push-off
- The trade-off between approach velocity, wall contact time, peak push-off force, and exit velocity is the central optimization problem

**Interpretation for the document:**
- "More knee bend = more push-off force" is not automatically true — timing matters more than magnitude
- The two techniques represent fundamentally different neuromuscular strategies (SSC-loaded vs. rapid impulse)
- Tuck Index (how compact the body is at rotation) is likely a key performance determinant
- This paper provides the kinetic grounding for the Section 5 push-off discussion
