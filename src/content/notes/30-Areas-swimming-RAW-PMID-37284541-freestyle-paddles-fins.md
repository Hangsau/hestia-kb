## Abstract (RAW — English only)

Effects of paddles (PAD) and fins (FINS) vs no-equipment (NE) in all-out front crawl exercises. 11 male regional-to-national swimmers. Results: FINS → higher velocity, larger stroke length (SL), lower kick amplitude, lower propulsion time. PAD → higher velocity vs NE. FINS → lower Index of Coordination (IdC < -1%, catch-up pattern). Both PAD and FINS → higher arm stroke efficiency (ηp) than NE. FINS → significantly higher estimated energy cost (C) vs NE and PAD. Key: equipment deeply modifies swimming stroke structure — kinematics, stroke efficiency, and coordination pattern. Coaches must scale equipment use to training objectives.

---

## Key Findings
1. **FINS**: higher velocity + larger SL + lower kick amplitude + lower propulsion time
2. **FINS**: catch-up coordination pattern (IdC < -1%) — slower inter-arm timing
3. **PAD and FINS both**: higher arm stroke efficiency (ηp) vs no equipment
4. **FINS**: higher energy cost (C) — swimming with fins is metabolically more expensive
5. **Equipment changes everything**: kinematics, efficiency, coordination — not just propulsion
