## Abstract

This study examined the effects of core training (CMT), inspiratory muscle training (IMT) and their combination (CIT) on strength and endurance of respiratory and core muscles, muscle oxygenation and swimming performance. Twenty-five competitive male swimmers, aged 15–20 years, were randomly assigned to one of three groups: CMT (n = 9), IMT (n = 8) at 60–80% of maximal inspiratory pressure (MIP), or CIT (n = 8), three times per week for 6 weeks alongside swimming training. MIP and inspiratory muscle endurance (IME), core strength and endurance, intercostals (IC) and vastus lateralis (VL) muscle oxygenation, and swimming performance were measured pre and post intervention. CIT and IMT significantly improved MIP and IME; core strength and endurance increased after CMT and CIT. Swimming performance improved in all groups, with the CIT group showing the largest effect size. The authors conclude that combined inspiratory and core training is the most effective approach for competitive swimmers.

## Technical Relevance

- **IMT (Inspiratory Muscle Training)**: Strengthens diaphragm and intercostals → improved respiratory endurance for longer races
- **Core strength**: Supports body roll in freestyle/backstroke, body undulation in butterfly
- **CIT combined approach**: Best outcomes for overall swimming performance
- Supports mechanism: respiratory fatigue → premature termination in high-intensity swimming
