## Abstract

Swimming-induced pulmonary edema (SIPE) is an acute condition affecting otherwise healthy athletes during immersion. Although incidence has been studied at sea level and low elevations, little is known about SIPE at moderate elevations. This was a prospective cross-sectional study among 7866 endurance athletes competing in half-ironman triathlons (St George, Utah, 771 m; Boulder, Colorado, 1665 m) and open-water swims.

Methods: Athletes underwent structured questionnaires, cardiopulmonary exams, and handheld lung ultrasound (LUS) using an adapted BLUE protocol. SIPE defined by ≥3 B lines in >1 lung field.

Key findings:
- 29/7866 athletes (0.37%) demonstrated LUS-confirmed SIPE
- Female incidence (0.68%) consistent with sea-level data
- Male incidence (0.26%) significantly higher than previously reported at 250 m (0.09%; P<.001)
- Nontraditional symptoms: anxiety, tachycardia, wetsuit tightness associated with several positive cases
- Mean SpO2 significantly lower among LUS-positive athletes (93.8 vs 95.5%; P=0.01)
- Revised diagnostic SpO2 threshold at altitude: ≤93% (vs standard ≤95% at sea level)
- Crackles: infrequent, low sensitivity (67%) but moderate specificity (86%)

Conclusions: SIPE incidence at moderate elevation is comparable overall to sea level but significantly higher in male athletes. Standard SpO2 diagnostic cutoffs may overestimate SIPE at altitude; ≤93% improves accuracy. Handheld LUS is feasible in endurance race settings.

## Technical Relevance

- 開放性水域游泳員應注意 SIPE 風險，即使在中等海拔（770-1665m）也與海平面發生率相當
- 男性在高海拔的 SIPE 發生率（0.26%）顯著高於低海拔（0.09%）——原因可能與血漿容積變化、血壓反應相關
- 傳統診斷標準（SpO2 ≤95%）在高山環境會高估 SIPE，需要調整至 ≤93%
- 非典型症狀（焦慮、心悸、潛水衣過緊）可能與 SIPE 相關——有症狀就要注意
- 肺部超聲波在賽事醫療站是可行的工具
