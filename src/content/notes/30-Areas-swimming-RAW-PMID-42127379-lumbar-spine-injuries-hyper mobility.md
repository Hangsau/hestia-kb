## Abstract

This retrospective study evaluated 285 competitive swimmers (mean age 18.7 ± 3.1 years) to identify risk factors for lumbar spine injuries. Twenty-nine swimmers (10.2%) reported lumbar spine injuries including spondylolysis, lumbar strain, disc pathology, and facet joint pain. Injured athletes were older, trained more hours in the pool, competed at a higher level, were hypermobile, and used kickboards less often. Adjusted logistic regression identified older age, higher competition level, and hypermobility as independent risk factors. The authors conclude that hypermobility screening should be incorporated into pre-participation evaluations for competitive swimmers.

## Technical Relevance

- **Hypermobility** → increased lumbar extension range → greater shear stress on lumbar vertebrae during freestyle/butterfly pull-through
- **Kickboard avoidance** = protective factor (reduces lumbar load by limiting sustained prone extension)
- Relevant to all strokes requiring lumbar extension: freestyle, butterfly, breaststroke kick
