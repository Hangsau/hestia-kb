## Abstract

The aim was to understand female swimmers' annual performance progression and stability between ages 10 and 18 across swimming distances and techniques. Data from female Portuguese Top-50 rankings in short-course pool extracted from swimrankings.net. Performances grouped by distances (50, 100, and 200 m) and techniques (freestyle, backstroke, breaststroke, butterfly), totalizing 12 events as performance metrics. A total of 343 swimmers and 3087 performances across nine consecutive competitive seasons were retrospectively assessed.

Methods: mean and normative stability computed for tracking performance trends; year-to-year percentage improvement reported; linear mixed-effects model using intraclass correlation coefficient (ICC) for differences across distances and techniques.

Key findings:
- Performance progression: marked improvements during early ages (up to 13% yearly)
- Emerging plateau around age 15-16 years
- Backstroke technique (ICC = 0.13) demonstrated greater consistency of individual differences on developmental trajectories
- Shorter races (50 m; ICC = 0.15) tended to be more stable than 100 m or 200 m (ICC = 0.12)

Conclusion: Female swimmers' performance stabilizes at 15-16 years. Despite reduced differences, backstroke and short distances show slightly more stable progression trends.

## Technical Relevance

- 15-16 歲為女性游泳員發展高原期的拐點
- 早期（10-13 歲）年進步幅度可達 13%，代表技術塑形與體能儲備的黃金期
- 背泳的發展軌跡最具一致性（ICC 0.13），可能與周期性動作特徵相關
- 短距離穩定性高於長距離，可能因為長距離更受耐力及策略因素影響
- 此研究支持：教學與訓練應在 13 歲前建立技術穩定性
