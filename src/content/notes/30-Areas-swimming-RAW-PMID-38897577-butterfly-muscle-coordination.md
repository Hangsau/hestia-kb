## Abstract (RAW — English only)

This study aimed to clarify the differences in muscular coordination during butterfly swimming between high- and low-performance swimmers using muscle synergy analysis. Eight female competitive swimmers and 8 female recreational swimmers participated. Participants swam a 25-m butterfly stroke with maximum effort. Surface electromyography was measured from 12 muscles and muscle synergy analysis was performed using nonnegative matrix factorization. Four synergies were extracted from both groups. Synergies 1 and 2 were characterized by coactivation of the upper and lower limb muscles in recreational swimmers, whereas only synergy 1 was characterized by this in competitive swimmers. Synergy 3 was involved in arm recovery in both groups. Synergy 4 was only involved in the downward kick in competitive swimmers. Competitive swimmers controlled the downward kick with an independent synergy. Adjustment of the timing of the downward kick may be an important factor for efficient butterfly swimming.

---

## Key Findings
1. **4 synergies extracted** in both groups
2. **Recreational swimmers**: Synergies 1 & 2 = upper + lower limb coactivation → less differentiated
3. **Competitive swimmers**: Synergy 1 alone = upper + lower limb coactivation → more efficient; Synergy 4 = independent downward kick control
4. **Key differentiator**: independent synergy for downward kick in competitive swimmers
5. **Training implication**: timing of downward kick is critical for butterfly efficiency
