## Abstract (RAW — English only)

### OBJECTIVE
The purpose of this study was to investigate the muscle activity patterns during the backstroke turn in age-group swimmers.

### METHODS
Thirty-two age-group swimmers performed backstroke turns. Surface electromyography (EMG) was used to record muscle activity from eight muscles during the turn. Turns were divided into approach, rotation, and push-off phases.

### RESULTS
Significant muscle activity differences were found between turn phases (p < 0.001). The push-off phase showed the highest activity in quadriceps, hamstrings, and gastrocnemius. The rotation phase showed high activity in abdominal muscles and hip flexors.

### CONCLUSIONS
Age-group swimmers should incorporate phase-specific strength training for backstroke turns. Training should emphasize quadriceps and gastrocnemius for push-off and abdominal/core stability for rotation.

---

## Key Findings
1. **Push-off phase**: highest activity in quadriceps, hamstrings, gastrocnemius
2. **Rotation phase**: high activity in abdominal muscles and hip flexors
3. **Phase-specific training**: quadriceps/gastrocnemius for push-off; abdominal/core for rotation
