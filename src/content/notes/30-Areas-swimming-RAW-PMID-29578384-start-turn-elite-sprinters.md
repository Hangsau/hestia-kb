# PMID-29578384 — Start and turn performances of elite sprinters at the 2016 European Championships in swimming

**Morais JE, Marinho DA, Arellano R, Barbosa TM** — Sports Biomech 2019 Feb;18(1):100-114

## Abstract

The aim was to examine the performance characteristics of male and female finalists in the 100-m distance at the 2016 European Championships. The performances of all 64 finalists (8 swimmers per event) were analysed across four strokes. Start and turn parameters were analysed.

**Key quantified findings:**
- Turn times by stroke (males): Freestyle 9.55 ± 0.13s (fastest), Butterfly, Backstroke, Breaststroke
- Turn times by stroke (females): Freestyle 10.78 ± 0.28s (fastest)
- Start + turn combined = **almost one-third of total race time** in all events
- **No significant differences** across the four swim strokes for start+turn combined contribution (p > 0.05)
- Start and turn contribution decreases for longer distance races (regression: decreasing effect of start for longer distances)
- Turn performance affects results **across all race distances** (p < 0.001)

**Interpretation for the document:**
- "Turns are 1/3 of race time" — this is the single strongest quantification of turn importance for Section 5
- Freestyle turns are fastest (flip turn advantage) — this is a technique advantage, not just a speed advantage
- The fact that start+turn share is similar across strokes means even slow-stroke swimmers (breaststroke) dedicate ~33% of race time to starts/turns
- Turn performance is a race-long determinant, not just a short-race variable — even 200m swimmers are significantly affected
