## Abstract (RAW — English only)

Extends prior findings on golden ratio and self-similar temporal patterns in walking/running and front-crawl/butterfly to breast-stroke and back-stroke. Generalized finite-length Fibonacci sequences describe self-similar patterns in gait/stroke subphases. Breast-stroke (highly technical, complex arm-leg coordination) and back-stroke (similar to front-crawl) were studied in international-level swimmers. Results confirm golden ratio patterns in stroke subphase durations for both strokes.

---

## Key Findings
1. **Golden ratio (φ ≈ 1.618) found in breaststroke and backstroke stroke subphases** — consistent with prior findings in front-crawl and butterfly
2. **Breaststroke**: complex arm-leg coordination still exhibits self-similar temporal patterns
3. **Backstroke**: similar temporal structure to front-crawl
4. **Self-similarity across stroke types**: elite swimmers' stroke subphases obey Fibonacci time harmonics
5. **Implication**: optimal stroke subphases may be constrained by biological time-harmonic structures
