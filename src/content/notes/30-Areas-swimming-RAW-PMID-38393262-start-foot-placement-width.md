## Abstract (RAW — English only)

### PURPOSE
This study aimed to investigate the effect of foot placement width on swimming start performance in skilled swimmers.

### METHODS
Nineteen skilled swimmers performed three trials of swimming starts using three foot placement conditions: narrow, medium, and wide stance. Block time, 2 m time, 5 m time, and total time were measured.

### RESULTS
The narrow foot placement significantly improved block time, 2 m time, and 5 m time compared to medium and wide conditions (p < 0.05). No significant difference was found between medium and wide conditions.

### CONCLUSION
These findings suggest that a narrow foot placement should be recommended to improve swimming start performance in skilled swimmers.

---

## Key Findings
1. **Narrow foot placement** significantly improved block time, 2m time, and 5m time vs medium and wide
2. **No significant difference** between medium and wide conditions
3. **Practical recommendation**: narrow stance for better start performance in skilled swimmers
