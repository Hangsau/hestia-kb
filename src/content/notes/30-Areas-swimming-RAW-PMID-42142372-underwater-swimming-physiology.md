## Abstract

In competition, swimmers alternate between swimming on the surface and underwater (e.g., after starts and turns). They train in apnea to improve tolerance to lack of oxygen during exercise, but research in this area remains limited. The aim was to investigate the impact of underwater swimming on physiological and perceptual responses of competitive swimmers.

Methods: Eighteen national-level swimmers (700.25 ± 53.42 AQUA points) performed 6 × 25m maximal swimming efforts from a push start with 3 minutes rest in three conditions:
1. Underwater (without breathing)
2. Surface (with normal breathing pattern)
3. Competitive (underwater + surface swimming segment)

Measured: swimming performance (T25), gases exchange, heart rate (HR), arterial oxygen saturation (SpO2), blood parameters, rating of perceived exertion (RPE), subjective feeling (FS).

Key findings:
- Underwater condition produced greater increase in blood lactate (p < 0.05, η² = 0.67) and potassium (p < 0.05, η² = 0.35) vs surface and competitive conditions
- No significant differences in HR, SpO2, or RPE
- Feeling scale (FS) values were LOWER (more unpleasant) in underwater condition
- Oxygen uptake (VO2) was significantly LOWER (p < 0.003, η² = 0.09) in underwater vs competitive swimming

Conclusion: A set of 25m underwater swimming efforts at maximum intensity generates intense muscular, perceptive, and metabolic demand in competitive swimmers, well tolerated by young trained competitive swimmers.

## Technical Relevance

- 乳酸效應值 η²=0.67 — 強度非常大，水面與水面+水下混合沒有這種效果
- 鉀離子上升代表肌肉細胞破壞/離子交換水下更明顯
- VO2 水下較低不代表效率高，而是因為在水下無法正常換氣所以有氧系統參與受限
- 「主觀感覺更差」但身體可承受——教練可利用這個特性設計水下間歇訓練
- 高血清鉀需要注意——這可能與心律不整風險相關
