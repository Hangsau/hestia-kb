## Abstract

Examined sex differences in three-dimensional intra-cycle velocity fluctuations (IVFs) of the body's center of mass and the relationship between IVFs and swimming velocity (Vswim) in high-level swimmers during freestyle. Maximal-effort freestyle sprints performed by 13 high-level athletes (7 males, 6 females) captured using a Qualisys three-dimensional motion capture system. Robust statistical approaches applied: Welch's t-tests, Bayesian analyses, permutation tests.

Key findings:
- Male swimmers exhibited significantly higher Vswim (p < 0.001, ES = 2.94), forward IVF (p < 0.001, ES = 2.47), and vertical IVF (p = 0.022, ES = 1.57) compared to female swimmers
- Bayesian analysis: NO statistically significant moderating effect of sex on the relationship between IVF and Vswim
- The functional relationship between IVF and performance remains statistically consistent across sexes

Practical implications:
- IVF-based interventions should integrate sex-related physiological characteristics with individualized technical profiling
- Avoid overgeneralization from male-based norms to female swimmers

Methodological contribution: Establishes high-fidelity 3D kinematic analysis reference and dataset for future research.

## Technical Relevance

- 男性划水速度顯著較高（ES 2.94），但速度波動模式本身與性能的關係在兩性間一致
- 這意味著「減少Intra-cycle velocity fluctuation」對男女同樣重要——目標一致，只是生理背景不同
- 垂直波動（vertical IVF）在男性更顯著——可能與划水深度、入水角度、性別體格差異相關
- 教練不應直接將男性划水技術研究結果應用於女性——需要性別調整過的技術基準
