# PMID-24149363 — Effects of distance specialization on the backstroke swimming kinematics

**Source:** J Sports Sci Med 2014

## Abstract

Purpose: Investigate biomechanical variables of backstroke technique in swimmers specialized in different distance events (50-m vs 200-m specialists), to understand how each group modifies arm stroke timing when velocity increases from sub-maximal to maximal.

Methods: Two 25-m backstroke trials at 70% and 100% maximum velocity. Nine 200-m distance swimmers and nine 50-m distance swimmers. Measured: swimming velocity, stroke length, stroke rate, duration of arm stroke phases, and kinematic variables.

**Key quantified findings:**
- In 50-m distance swimmers: duration of propulsive phase at Vmax (expressed as % of total underwater arm stroke) **increased significantly (p = 0.001)** with increasing velocity
- Both pull and push phases were fundamental in the increase of propulsive phase duration
- 50-m specialists more able to modify arm stroke phases duration when increasing velocity vs 200-m specialists
- 200-m specialists maintain more consistent stroke pattern across velocities (higher SF at race pace, lower SF at submax)

**Interpretation:**
- Sprint specialists (50m) have greater **modifiability** of stroke timing with velocity — they can shift more work to propulsive phases at high speed
- Distance swimmers (200m) maintain consistent SF/SL ratio regardless of pace (metabolic economy priority)
- Backstroke speed increase requires proportionally longer propulsive phases (pull + push) — not just higher SF

**教練意涵：**
- 50m 訓練重點：推進時相的時間延伸能力
- 200m 訓練重點：維持推進效率的穩定性
- 兩者在划手時序上有不同的優化方向
