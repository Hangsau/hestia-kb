## Abstract (RAW — English only)

This study investigated the essential role of the kicking action in front crawl. Relationships of hand propulsive force and trunk inclination with swimming velocity were examined from 0.75 m/s to maximum effort. Three conditions: arm stroke with pull buoy (PB), arm stroke without pull buoy (AWOB), arm stroke with six-beat kick (SWIM). Key results: AWOB consistently showed greater hand propulsive force than SWIM across velocities (p < 0.05) and greater trunk inclination below severe velocity (p < 0.05). These differences increased with decreasing velocity. Conclusions: (1) Kicking action reduces pressure drag on the trunk, allowing swimmers to achieve a given velocity with less hand propulsive force. (2) This effect is significant in low-velocity ranges.

---

## Key Findings
1. **Kicking reduces pressure drag on trunk** — allows same velocity with less hand propulsion
2. **Effect most significant at low velocities** — kick's drag reduction matters more at slower speeds
3. **AWOB > SWIM for hand propulsive force** at all speeds — meaning the kick is doing something that reduces the need for arm propulsion
4. **AWOB > SWIM for trunk inclination** at lower speeds — kick keeps body more horizontal
5. **Mechanism: drag reduction via horizontal body position** — not propulsion contribution per se

### Integration points:
- **踢腿的首要功能是減少阻力**：不是直接推進，而是讓身體保持水平，減少前進方向的水動阻力
- **低速時踢腿效果更明顯**：在慢速/暖身/復健時，踢腿的姿態維持更關鍵
- **踢腿不等於推進力**：划水時若踢腿過度用力，可能增加無效的身體起伏，反而不利
- **划幅/划频優化要同时考虑踢腿**: 在高速度区间 kick 的阻力减少效果变低，此时手臂推进成为主导
