## Abstract (RAW — English only)

Purpose: To investigate the relationship between anthropometric characteristics, biomechanical variables and performance in young and adolescent swimmers. Database search from January 2001 to June 2021, 43 studies selected. Manuscripts divided by stroke: butterfly, backstroke, breaststroke, front crawl. Results showed the importance of anthropometric variables for performance. For **front crawl**: consensus that higher height and arm span, along with high body mass and lean body mass, contribute positively to better stroke length and stroke index values. For other strokes: lack of common variables across studies.

---

## Key Findings
1. **Front crawl**: consensus — height + arm span + body mass + lean body mass → better stroke length & stroke index
2. **Other strokes (butterfly, backstroke, breaststroke)**: lack of consistent anthropometric predictors across studies
3. **Adolescent swimmers**: anthropometrics matter but stroke-specific relationships differ
