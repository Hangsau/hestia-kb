## Abstract (RAW — English only)

Aimed to: (1) quantify performance development of race times and KPIs of European swimmers across 2016-2021 Olympic cycle, and (2) provide reference values for long-course events. 246 men + 256 women finalists at European championships. Key findings: start times improved in 29/32 events. For alternating strokes (backstroke, freestyle): improved swimming efficiency — reduced stroke rate + increased distance per stroke. Breakout distances increased. Clean swimming velocities faster in 17/32 events. 100m and 200m events showed most improvement in males; breaststroke and sprint events in females.

---

## Key Findings
1. **Start improvement widespread**: 29/32 events improved start times
2. **Alternating strokes (freestyle, backstroke)**: reduced SR + increased distance per stroke → improved efficiency
3. **Breakout distances increased**: swimmers going further underwater after start/turn
4. **Clean swimming velocities**: faster in 17/32 events
5. **Distance-specific trends**: males improved most in 100m/200m; females in BR and sprints
6. **Turn times**: inconclusive improvements — turns remain variable
