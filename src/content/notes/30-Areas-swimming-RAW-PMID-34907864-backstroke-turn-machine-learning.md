## Abstract (RAW — English only)

### OBJECTIVE
To predict backstroke turn performance in age-group swimmers using machine learning and to identify the most important input variables for each turn technique.

### METHODS
One hundred twenty age-group swimmers performed backstroke turns using open, somersault, bucket, and crossover techniques. Sixty-four kinematic, temporal, and kinetic variables were measured. Lasso regression, ridge regression, and AdaBoost were used to predict turn time.

### RESULTS
AdaBoost showed the best predictive performance (R² = 0.644–0.825). The most important variables differed by technique: for open turns, time to peak torque of hip abductors; for somersault turns, horizontal velocity at hand enter; for bucket turns, ankle range of motion; for crossover turns, time to peak torque of knee extensors.

### CONCLUSIONS
Machine learning can effectively predict backstroke turn performance. Each turn technique has unique performance predictors, suggesting individualized training approaches.

## Key Findings
1. **AdaBoost best**: R² = 0.644–0.825 for turn time prediction
2. **Different predictors per technique**:
   - Open: time to peak torque of hip abductors
   - Somersault: horizontal velocity at hand enter
   - Bucket: ankle range of motion
   - Crossover: time to peak torque of knee extensors
3. **Implication**: each turn technique has unique performance drivers → individualized training
