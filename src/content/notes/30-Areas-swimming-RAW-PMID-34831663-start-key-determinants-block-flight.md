## Abstract (RAW — English only)

### OBJECTIVE
To investigate the key determinants of swimming start performance by analyzing block and flight phases in elite sprinters.

### METHODS
Fourteen male elite sprint swimmers performed maximal effort starts. Three-dimensional motion capture and force platform data were collected. Block phase (backward and forward forces, time to peak force, block time, and take-off angle) and flight phase (flight time, horizontal distance, and horizontal velocity) were analyzed. Correlations and multiple regression were used to identify key determinants.

### RESULTS
Block phase explained 64% of the variance in 5 m swim time, and flight phase explained 65%. Back knee angle at pool edge, block time, and take-off angle were the most influential variables.

### CONCLUSIONS
Both block and flight phases are critical for start performance. Training should target both phases, with emphasis on rear knee angle and block phase force production.

## Key Findings
1. **Block phase**: explains 64% of variance in 5m swim time
2. **Flight phase**: explains 65% of variance in 5m swim time
3. **Most influential variables**: back knee angle at pool edge, block time, take-off angle
4. **Both phases critical**: training should address both block phase force production AND flight phase execution
