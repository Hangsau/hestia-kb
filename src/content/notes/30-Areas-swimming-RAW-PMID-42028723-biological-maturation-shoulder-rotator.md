## Abstract

This study investigated the effect of biological maturation on shoulder rotator strength and swimming performance after a 6-week summer break in young male swimmers. Forty-two young male swimmers (age 12–16) were grouped by maturation status (pre-peak height velocity [PHV] vs. at/near PHV). Isokinetic strength of internal and external shoulder rotators (at 60°/s and 180°/s) and 100m freestyle performance were measured before and after the break. Post-break assessments revealed that external rotator strength decreased significantly more in the at/near PHV group (p < 0.01), while internal rotator strength was maintained. Swimming performance showed greater decline in the more mature group. The authors suggest that swimmers at or near PHV require specific maintenance programs during training breaks to preserve shoulder stability and performance.

## Technical Relevance

- **PHV (Peak Height Velocity)**: Period of rapid growth associated with decreased shoulder stabilizer strength
- External rotators (infraspinatus, teres minor): Critical for catch phase humeral rotation positioning
- Practical implication: Coaches should prescribe specific rotator cuff maintenance during rest periods, especially for maturing swimmers
