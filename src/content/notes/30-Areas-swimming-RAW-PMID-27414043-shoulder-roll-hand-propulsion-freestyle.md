# PMID-27414043 — Relationship between shoulder roll and hand propulsion in the front crawl stroke

**Kudo S, Sakurai Y, Miwa T, Matsuda Y** — J Sports Sci 2017 May;35(10):945-952

## Abstract

This study re-evaluated the magnitude of hand propulsion (HP) in the pull and push phases of the front crawl stroke and investigated the association between the angular velocity of shoulder roll (ωSR) and hand propulsive lift (HPL). ωSR was computed for 16 skilled swimmers performing front crawl at maximal sprinting pace. HP, hand propulsive drag (HPD) and HPL were determined by a dynamic pressure approach.

**Key findings:**
- HP and HPD in the **pull phase** were **greater than** in the push phase (P < 0.05)
- HPL in the pull phase was **similar** to that in the push phase
- 11 out of 16 swimmers had a **significant correlation between ωSR and HPL in the push phase** (P < 0.05): HPL increased in the push phase as the ωSR of rolling back to the neutral position became faster
- "A swimmer should use more drag for hand propulsion in the pull phase and propulsion from drag and lift equally in the push phase"

**Interpretation for the document:**
- The pull-push boundary in freestyle is not just a geometric distinction — it has distinct propulsion mechanisms
- **Pull phase = drag-dominant** (hand moving backward relative to water, directly pushing)
- **Push phase = drag + lift equally** — the lift component requires body roll to create the angle of attack
- Faster shoulder roll in the push phase = more lift propulsion = more total HP
- The "squeeze" feeling at the end of the pull is the lift-assisted push — body roll enables it

**Keywords:** front crawl, shoulder roll, hand propulsion, drag, lift, biomechanics
